IBM Professional Data Science Certificate -- Applied Data Science Capstone -- Course Project

Background Scenario
During the commercial space age, space travel will be more affordable, and Virgin Galactic is providing suborbital spaceflights to the public.
Rocket Lab is instead a small satellite provider, and Blue Origin manufactures sub-orbital reusable rockets. Last, SpaceX has already sent spacecraft to the International
Space Station, and Starlink, a satellite internet constellation that provides satellite Internet access, has sent manned missions to space. SpaceX can do this, because
rocket launches are relatively inexpensive when compared to the past, and SpaceX advertises Falcon 9 rocket launches that cost 62 million dollars on its website, while
other providers charge up to 165 million dollars each, so SpaceX can reuse the first stage with those savings. If we can determine if the first stage will land, we can thus
determine how much a launch will cost.

Project Overview
SpaceX's Falcon 9 launches like a regular rocket, so we'll use diagrams from Forest Katsch, a 3D artists and softwaare engineer, from zlsadesign.com. He makes infographics and
art regarding space in addition to software. The payload is enclosed in the fairings. Stage 2 brings the payload to orbit, but most of the work is completed during the first
stage. 

For this project, SpaceY would like to compete with SpaceX and is founded by billionaire industrialist Allon Musk. We will determine the price of each launch by gathering
information about SpaceX and creating dashboards for the team. We will additionally determine if SpaceX will reuse the first stage, but instead of using rocket science to
determine if the first stage will land successfully, we will train a machine learning model and use public information to predict this.
